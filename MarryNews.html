14-Year-Old Girl Found Dead at Home; Questions Remain Over Possible Outside Influence

Investigators say the death of Marry Brown, confirmed as suicide by wrist laceration, has left her family devastated and prompted broader concern about hidden online influence.

Local Desk New York, NY Updated March 27, 2026 CityWatch 12 Staff

New York, NY

A 14-year-old girl was found dead at home in what authorities have confirmed as a suicide by wrist laceration.

According to her parents, the girl had shown no prior signs of self-harm behavior. They stated that she had never exhibited tendencies toward harming herself, making the incident both sudden and incomprehensible to the family.

Investigators noted that the method used appeared unusually precise and deliberate, suggesting a level of familiarity not typically expected. Authorities have not ruled out the possibility of external influence or instruction.

The parents are reported to be in extreme distress following the incident. In statements given to local media, they described their daughter as “quiet, sensitive, and well-behaved,” and expressed deep grief and confusion over her sudden death.

“We don’t understand how this could happen,” they said. “There were no signs. Nothing that could have prepared us for this.”

The case has raised concerns among local authorities, who are urging parents to pay closer attention to the mental health and online activity of adolescents.
