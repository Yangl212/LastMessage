March 20 • New York

On March 20, a student death was reported at Riverside High School. A 16-year-old female student was found injured in a classroom on campus and was later confirmed deceased.

According to a statement released by the school, the incident occurred shortly after classes had ended that afternoon. The student was discovered during a routine check, and school staff immediately contacted the school nurse and notified the police. Emergency responders arrived at the scene and confirmed that the student showed no signs of life.

Police stated that preliminary findings suggest the case to be an act of self-harm. The investigation remains ongoing, and no evidence of external physical assault has been found at this time.

In response to various claims circulating online and among students, the school noted that opinions regarding the student’s personal behavior and relationships have been mixed. However, initial reviews indicate that most of these claims are unverified rumors, or stem from misunderstandings and emotional disputes among students.

Riverside High School has initiated psychological support measures to assist affected students and stated that it will continue to cooperate with authorities throughout the investigation. The public is urged to approach the situation responsibly, refrain from spreading unverified information, and respect the privacy of the deceased and her family.

Further Details

Officials have not released additional personal details at this stage. The campus remains under review, and interviews with students and staff are continuing.

The school reported that support services will remain available as authorities complete the investigation.
